Our Programs

Assessment: Each player undergoes an initial/base skill assessment to identify strengths and areas for improvement.

THE SMART APPROACH

Specific - Specific goals, expectations and outcomes regarding player's needs.

Measurable - Measuring the player's objectives determines if the player is making progress. It will be kept on track and on schedule.

Achievable - Realistic goals must be set so success can be achieved.

Relevant - Everything we do will relate to football skills development; it must be relevant to the player's needs.

Time-bound - Every objective has a specific timeline for completion.

Tailored Plans: Custom training plans are designed to meet the specific needs and goals of each player. SMART approach is adopted in formulating tailored plans for individual players based on their current needs. The programmes are inclusive of Short-Term, Mid-Term and Long-Term aims for each player. The focus of the programme is on a continuous evaluative and reflective programme in nature. The programme adopts the following levels and age specific expected skills:

  • Fundamentals: Normally 7-8 years. Players must enjoy the practice of physical activity, developing fundamental motor skills (passing, controlling, running with the ball, jumping, coordination, agility, balance, throwing, shooting), all these skills are done in a fun-playful environment.
  • Basics of the Game: Normally 8-10 years. Players improve their BCAs (Balance, Coordination and Agility) and fundamental motor skills in a less playful environment, discipline is instilled. Position structures and tactics are introduced.
  • Developing to know: Normally 10-12 years. Advanced technical skills and tactical principles are instilled.   • Reaching the Elite Level: 13-15 years and older. High level of fitness is important, game intelligence is key for good performances, which means technically and tactically players must be on their A game. Transition must be understood; Professional Football Mindset must be applied to reach that level. Skill Development Sessions

Focused training on fundamental skills such as dribbling, passing, shooting, and defending, ensuring a strong foundation.

Technique Correction

Dedicated sessions to address and refine individual techniques, helping players to improve their execution and overall performance.

Tactical Training

In-depth education on game strategies, positioning, and decision-making to enhance players' understanding of the game.

Group Training Sessions

Opportunities for players to train in small groups, fostering teamwork while still focusing on individual development. Small-sided games for players to have more touches on the ball, make decisions and mistakes so they can develop.

Goalkeeper Specific Training

Tailored programs for goalkeepers that focus on positioning, shot-stopping techniques, distribution and how to communicate as the eye for your team.

Psychological- Emotional training

Psychology training helps footballers train with more focus, play with more confidence, and reflect more effectively after a match. Players learn to control their emotions.

Communications training

Terminology; Body Language; Listen to your teammates; Tone of voice; Understand the individuals in your group; Ask for help; Plan for your communication.

Performance Analysis

Use of video analysis and performance metrics to provide feedback and track progress, helping players understand their development journey.
